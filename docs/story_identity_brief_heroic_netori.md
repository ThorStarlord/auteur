# Story Identity Brief: Heroic Netori Dual-POV Genre Inversion

This brief defines a high-complexity dual-POV story archetype that uses perspective shifts to deliver a calculated genre inversion (NTR-mystery shell to heroic netori payoff).

## Core Answer
A high-complexity dual-POV genre inversion built as **heroic netori** with an **ntr_mystery** false-protagonist shell. The first POV creates investigative tension but exposes his own claim as illegitimate, allowing the second POV to pay off as romantic liberation.

---

## 1. Target Experience (Layer 1)
- **Primary Emotional Promise**: `reframing satisfaction`
- **Emotional Progression**: `paranoia -> suspicion -> moral unease -> revelation -> liberation/conquest satisfaction`
- **Secondary Palette**:
  - `mystery tension`
  - `moral discomfort`
  - `romantic liberation`
  - `rival-collapse satisfaction`
- **Avoided Outcomes**:
  - `pure victim tragedy`
  - `clean cheating melodrama`
  - `sympathy lock on the false MC`

---

## 2. Promise / Constraints (Layer 2)
- **Medium**: `novel` or `visual novel`
- **Mode**: `tragic / conquest`
- **Genre**: `heroic_netori` (Dominant)
- **Subgenres**:
  - `ntr_mystery` (Secondary / Misdirection shell)
  - `romantic_liberation` (Tertiary / Legitimacy payoff)
- **What This Is Not**:
  - Making the false MC fully sympathetic and adequate, because that turns the ending into NTR tragedy.
  - Making the true MC purely dominant without moral legitimacy, because that changes heroic netori into villainous netori.
  - Making the heroine passive, because heroic netori requires her authentic choice.
  - Solving the mystery only through a twist, because the audience needs fair recontextualization clues.
  - Adding many extra genres before the POV inversion is stable.

---

## 3. Central Engine Forces (Layer 4)
### False MC (First POV / Misdirection Shell)
- **Want**: He wants proof that his relationship is being taken from him.
- **Resistance**: He cannot accept that his own inadequacy may be the cause of her distance.
- **Conflict**: His search for proof slowly reveals evidence against his own legitimacy.
- **Stakes**: If the truth is misread, liberation becomes betrayal; if the true MC fails, she remains trapped.
- **Change**: From apparent victim to exposed unworthy holder.

### True MC (Second POV / Reveal)
- **Want**: He wants to free the heroine from a bond he believes is illegitimate.
- **Resistance**: The heroine is bound by obligation, guilt, or social pressure.
- **Conflict**: To save her, he must violate the social rule that prior claim deserves respect.
- **Stakes**: If he fails, she remains trapped in an invalid relationship.
- **Change**: From outsider/interloper to rightful liberator.

### Heroine
- **Change**: From obligated partner to conscious chooser.

---

## 4. Scope Contract (Layer 3)
- **Minimum**: `novella`
- **Recommended**: `novel` or `visual novel`
- **POV Budget**: 2
- **Subplot Budget**: 1
- **Warning**: Do not add extra major POVs until the core reversal works.

---

## 5. Recommendation Contract
- **Mode**: `opinionated`
- **Best Basis**: `genre_aligned`
- **Why This Is Best**: Equal 50/50 weighting of the two genres will break the audience contract and cause extreme emotional whiplash. By enforcing a clear hierarchy where heroic netori is the dominant contract and NTR-mystery is the opening misdirection mask, Auteur guides the author to build a controlled audience-position flip that feels earned and satisfying.
- **Rejected Directions**:
  - Making the false MC fully loving, adequate, and innocent, which collapses the story into a standard tragic NTR.
  - Treating both POVs as equally sympathetic, which fractures reader identification and leads to audience whiplash.
