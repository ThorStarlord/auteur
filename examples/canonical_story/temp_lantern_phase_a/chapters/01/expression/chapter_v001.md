# The dark beacon

Mara climbed until the river wind found the gap in her coat. Below, the water
had withdrawn from the stones, leaving the old nails bright as fish bones. The
lantern waited above her, empty.
<!-- auteur:end-scene id=scene_01_01 -->
<!-- auteur:transition id=transition_01_02 revision=1 -->
The river wind carries Tomas's warning up the tower.
<!-- auteur:end-transition id=transition_01_02 -->
<!-- auteur:scene id=scene_01_02 expression_revision=1 -->
# The messenger

Tomas reached the tower without breath. A boat had grounded below the bend,
and the magistrate was on it. Mara put one hand on the lantern door and heard
the river answer beneath them.
<!-- auteur:end-scene id=scene_01_02 -->

<!-- auteur:scene id=scene_01_03 expression_revision=1 -->
# The ledger

The ledger smelled of river mud. Mara found her brother's name between two
debts, written in the magistrate's narrow hand.
<!-- auteur:end-scene id=scene_01_03 -->

<!-- auteur:scene id=scene_01_04 expression_revision=1 -->
# The light

Mara struck the match. The beacon opened its eye above the black water, and
the boat turned.
<!-- auteur:end-scene id=scene_01_04 -->

<!-- auteur:scene id=scene_01_05 expression_revision=1 -->
# No thanks

At dawn the magistrate offered payment. Mara refused it, kept the ledger, and
asked Tomas to help repair the tower before the next flood.
<!-- auteur:end-scene id=scene_01_05 -->
