# Blueprint: The Lantern at Low Water

Mara wants to protect the river town without becoming an instrument of the
magistrate's violence. The beacon is both a practical signal and the story's
central image of responsibility.

One chapter contains five scenes:

1. Mara finds the old beacon dark at low water.
2. A messenger brings word that the magistrate's boat is sinking.
3. Mara discovers her brother's name on the condemnation ledger.
4. She lights the beacon despite the chance to let the boat pass.
5. At dawn, she refuses the magistrate's thanks and keeps the ledger.
